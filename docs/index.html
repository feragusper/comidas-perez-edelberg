The week's menu, sorted.

A private meal planner for a family of three. It turns our household rules — dinner leftovers become tomorrow's lunch, some meals are keto, and there's what the little one can and can't eat — into a weekly plan that just works.

Open the app View on GitHub
The motivation

Why it exists

We're a family of three, with a small child. Deciding what to eat every day is a puzzle with rules: dinner leftovers get eaten for lunch the next day, some meals are keto, and there are things the little one can and can't eat yet. Planning the week around those rules solves the daily grind — and lets us keep a backlog of ideas based on what's already in the pantry, so we shop only for what's missing.

Functional requirements

What it does

Five sections, each solving a piece of the eat-at-home cycle.

📅

Weekly menu

Fill in dinner, breakfast and snack for the whole week, honoring the family rules (leftovers as next-day lunch, keto, kid-friendly). Mixes your history with fresh AI ideas. Reorder with drag & drop and move between weeks.

🍲

My meals

Your own meal catalog: name, icon and the ingredients that make it up. Tags (keto, etc.) and sorting by usage.

🏠

Don Bacilio (pantry)

Track what you have at home and ask the AI what to cook with it — without a shopping trip.

🛒

Shopping

A shopping list built from the menu, grouped by category, with checkoff and copy to clipboard.

📊

Reports

Analytics on your habits: most-used meals, keto ratio, weekly trends with charts.

Non-functional requirements

How it behaves

  • Mobile-first. Built for the phone, with adapted navigation and on-screen keyboard handling.
  • Authentication. Access protected by login (Supabase Auth + Google via Lovable).
  • Multi-environment. Stage and prod share one database, split by key prefix.
  • AI with limits. Suggestions with rate-limit and credit handling, with clear messages.
  • Immutable history. Each week stores a snapshot that is never rewritten.
  • Continuous deploy. Changes integrated and published automatically via Lovable.
Under the hood

Architecture

A client-side SPA against a managed backend (Supabase). The AI lives in edge functions.

🖥️ Client (SPA)
React + TypeScript + Vite. Server state with TanStack Query, routing with React Router.
↕
🔐 Supabase Auth
Login and session. Protected routes on the client.
🗄️ Postgres
Meals, ingredients, pantry and weekly menu snapshots.
⚡ Edge Functions
AI suggestions with rate-limit and credits.
📌 Data model: meals are combinations of ingredients. The weekly plan stores a snapshot resolved by id against the live catalog at read time, so history never breaks when you edit a meal.
